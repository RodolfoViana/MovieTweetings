Oscar Movie Tweetings

An Analysis of the Oscars Nominated With a Live Movie Rating Datase

Genesis

When a movie get nominations it happens an “Oscar bump”, when nominations raise its profile and thousands of moviegoers check it out to see what the fuss is about. Even the best films will always have 1-2% of IMDb voters giving it the lowest score of “1” as if it was a completed waste of time. The number of “1”s scores for some oscar nominations is really high, that is why Ryan Adams like to call “Hate-Rate”. Inspired by Ryan, we show the "Oscar bump" effect.

All We Need is Data

To collect data we use MovieTweetings. Which is consisting of ratings on movies that were contained in well-structured tweets on Twitter. This dataset is the result of research conducted by Simon Dooms (Ghent University, Belgium) and has been presented on the CrowdRec 2013 workshop which is co-located with the ACM RecSys 2013 conference. With the addition of around 500 new ratings per day, this dataset can be very useful as an always up-to-date and natural rating dataset.

The Analysis

The purpose of this analysis is to make a comparison between the films nominated for an Academy Award, in order to check for discrepancies between the views of the public before and after oscar nomination.



Haters Going to Hate

In 2013 and 2014 all movies nominated to best picture got highest rating BEFORE being nominated



Oscar Bump

When we look at 3 months before the oscar nominated (Jan) we can see that almost all movies gets an really low score. In 2013 "Her" was the "champion Hate-Rate" and in 2014 was "American Sniper"
